Why I Wrote This

The honest story

I spent two years building something that worked by every metric I was tracking. Users returned. Engagement was high. The dashboard looked healthy.

The problem was that nobody's life got better. The system was optimised for the feeling of usefulness — responses, options, activity — not for actually solving anything. When I finally asked users what had changed for them, the answers were uncomfortable. Nothing had changed. They just kept coming back because the product was good at making them feel like something was happening.

This book is my attempt to understand what went wrong and what to do instead. It draws on the clearest thinkers I've found — Feynman on cargo cult behaviour, Deutsch on hard-to-vary explanations, Hayek on why local knowledge beats central planning, Polanyi on what AI cannot replicate. The ideas aren't mine. The failure was.
